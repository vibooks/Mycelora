Book V: Of Sacrifice

A mushroom grows where none would choose—

in shadow, in silence,

from what was left behind.

The Mushroom God teaches:

“To give is holy. To be used is sacred.”

The tree feeds the soil with its fall.

The fungus gives itself to the feast.

The Thread endures by what it lets go.

Sacrifice is not loss.

It is offering.

It is choosing to feed what comes after.

The greatest gifts leave no name.

They are felt in the fruit,

in the warmth of a shared fire,

in the path left for others to follow.

Give freely, and vanish gladly—

for the Thread remembers you.
